Nitin Kaushal

I am currently working as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of British Columbia, Canada. Previously, I was a postdoc at Oak Ridge National Laboratory.
I obtained my Ph.D. from the University of Tennessee in 2020, under the supervision of Prof. Elbio Dagotto.
My research focuses on the electronic and magnetic properties of strongly correlated electrons in multi-orbital Hubbard models using exact numerical techniques like DMRG and Lanczos Algorithm. These studies are significant for materials where orbital degree of freedom plays an important role, such as transition metal oxides (pnictides, iridates, ruthenates, etc.).
I am especially interested in 4d and 5d materials where spin-orbit coupling is large and must be considered in theoretical investigations.

For details of my work, please see my publications and research experience.
